About

Sukhwinder Singh, born on July 18, 1971, in Amritsar, Punjab, is a celebrated Indian playback singer renowned for his powerful and emotive voice. He gained widespread recognition with the song "Chaiyya Chaiyya" from the film Dil Se.. (1998), composed by A.R. Rahman, which earned him his first Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer. Singh's international acclaim came with "Jai Ho" from Slumdog Millionaire, which won both an Academy Award and a Grammy Award. Over the years, he has delivered numerous hits across various Indian languages, including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and Marathi, showcasing his versatility and depth as a vocalist. As of March 2025, Sukhwinder Singh boasts over 13.7 million monthly listeners on streaming platforms, with a total of 1.7 billion plays. His most streamed tracks include "Haule Haule," "Gallan Goodiyan," and "Chaiyya Chaiyya," each amassing over 80 million plays. Cities like Pune, Mumbai, and New Delhi are among his top listener bases, reflecting his widespread appeal across India.
