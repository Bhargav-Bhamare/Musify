About

Sonu Nigam, born on July 30, 1973, in Faridabad, Haryana, is a renowned Indian playback singer, music director, and actor. Often hailed as one of the most versatile and influential voices in Indian music, Nigam has recorded over 6,000 songs across more than 32 languages, including Hindi, Kannada, Bengali, Marathi, and Tamil. His career began in the early 1990s, and he gained widespread recognition with hits like "Sandese Aate Hai" from Border and "Kal Ho Naa Ho" from the film of the same name. His ability to adapt to various musical genres, from romantic ballads to classical and devotional songs, has earned him numerous accolades, including the National Film Award, multiple Filmfare Awards, and the prestigious Padma Shri in 2022 . As of March 2025, Sonu Nigam boasts over 20 million monthly listeners on streaming platforms, with a total of 4.1 billion plays . His most streamed tracks include "Main Agar Kahoon," "Kal Ho Naa Ho," and "Abhi Mujh Mein Kahin," each amassing over 90 million plays. Cities like Bengaluru, Pune, and Mumbai are among his top listener bases, reflecting his widespread appeal across India. Beyond studio recordings, Nigam is celebrated for his dynamic live performances and has served as a judge on various music reality shows, further cementing his status as a mentor and inspiration to aspiring artists .
