About

Pritam Chakraborty, born on June 14, 1971, in Kolkata, is a celebrated Indian music composer, instrumentalist, and producer renowned for his contributions to Bollywood music. Trained in music by his father, Prabodh Chakraborty, who ran a music school, Pritam pursued a degree in geology from Presidency College, Kolkata, and later studied sound engineering at the Film and Television Institute of India (FTII) in Pune. He began his career composing ad jingles before making his Bollywood debut with the film Tere Liye in 2001. His breakthrough came with the 2004 film Dhoom, whose title track became a massive hit. Over the past two decades, Pritam has composed music for over 125 Bollywood films, earning numerous accolades, including a National Film Award in 2022 for Brahmāstra: Part One – Shiva, six Filmfare Awards, and multiple IIFA and Zee Cine Awards . As of May 2025, Pritam boasts over 42 million monthly listeners on Spotify, ranking him among the top 100 artists globally . His most streamed tracks include "Haan Tum Ho," "Chhan Ke Mohalla," and "Tu Hi Toh Hai," each garnering millions of plays . Pritam's music resonates with audiences worldwide, reflecting his ability to blend Indian classical elements with contemporary sounds. Beyond composing, he founded JAM8 in 2016, an A\&R music production platform that nurtures emerging talent and contributes to various Bollywood projects . His enduring influence and innovative approach continue to shape the landscape of Indian film music.
